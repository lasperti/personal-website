This is a log regarding a mandatory class I took in my University: MAC0102 - Paths in the Bachelor of Computer Science.

The objective of the course is to help second-year students map their academic trajectories inside the Institute.

After attending those classes, here is my resulting plan.

Before I entered IME-USP, I had a defined plan: operate in offensive cybersecurity, both as a technical researcher and as a leader. This dictated my decision to join IMEsec in 2025, and to now guide it as President.

The remaining problem was the academic path choice: which combination of classes are more adequate for this objective?

As I dove deeper inside my cybersecurity studies, it became evident that isolated knowledge is a vulnerability.

  • When reverse engineering a binary with a database, you have to understand SQL architecture.
  • When exploiting a cryptographic algorithm, you have to map elliptic curves.
  • When auditing a smart contract, you have to parse its linear optimization models.

The takeaway is: to exploit a protocol, you must first understand its foundational logic.

For this reason, I will follow the Theoretical Computer Science track to its completion, taking classes such as MAC0315 - Linear Optimization and MAC0336 - Cryptography for Data Security, while selectively taking Software Systems classes like MAC0332 - Software Engineering and MAC0470 - Free and Open Source Software Development.

I am optimizing for friction. The goal is to force new mental models, building the absolute cognitive adaptability required to map, approach, and dismantle any system I encounter, regardless of its underlying architecture.
